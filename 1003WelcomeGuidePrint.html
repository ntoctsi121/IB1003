Getting around.
Taxis · Drivers · Rentals
West Bay → Coxen Hole
The short version

If you plan on staying on West Bay Beach to relax, swim, snorkel and dive, you definitely don't need a car. But if you're the type that likes to explore, there's plenty to see around the rest of the island.

Taxis and private drivers can take you all around — though with a big group that adds up. You can rent a car from the airport, from West Bay Mall down the road, or from the rental desk right inside the entrance to Infinity Bay. The resort also offers transportation to and from the airport for $15 roundtrip per person.

Where to eat & drink.
18 SPOTS, HAND-PICKED WEST BAY · WEST END
An island truth

There are more restaurants than you'll ever need within walking distance. We also recommend a water taxi to West End to check out the places there. You really can't go wrong — and food & drinks here are cheaper than most other islands we've traveled to.

Pro tip

For small, popular spots (Lily Pond Garden, The Drunken Sailor, Kristi's), reserve ahead or go off-peak. WhatsApp or Facebook is usually the easiest way to book.

House favorite
01 · Right next door · West Bay

Bananarama.

Right next door on the beach. Super casual and very reasonably priced, with 2-for-1 food & drink specials posted daily on a whiteboard out front. Pizza for picky family members; live music and karaoke at night. Show up in swimsuits — the kids play in the sand while your food cooks.

our kids beg to go back to Roatán just for these drinks.
Full West End view
02 · West End

Mila del Mar.

Perched right over the water in West End with gorgeous views across the bay. The food is fantastic and the cocktails are wonderful — grab a table at the rail, order a drink, and watch the boats come and go. Worth the water-taxi trip all on its own.

the view is the appetizer — come hungry anyway.
Where we got married
03 · Lighthouse Point · 15-min uphill walk

Kismet.

At the Meridian at Lighthouse Point — a development up the hill past Infinity Bay with a restaurant at the westernmost point of the island. Open for lunch and dinner. Very secluded, especially nice on cruise-ship days when West Bay gets crowded.

we actually got married here. it's that pretty.

A short stroll down the beach in West Bay, with tables right on the sand and one of the prettiest sunset views around. Known for its steaks and seafood — get the whole grilled fish, you won't regret it. Time it for golden hour.

Currency · Tipping
Cards · Tax
Currency · Conversion

The US Dollar works everywhere.

No need to stress about exchanging money before you arrive. Occasionally prices are listed in Honduran Lempiras, but vendors still take USD.

Heads-up: if your dollar bill has even a small tear, it may be rejected — bring crisp bills if you can.

The rate, roughly
26L ≈ $1 USD

So something priced at 100L is about $4. Or open the iPhone Calculator → tap the icon → toggle Convert.

Credit cards

Bring Visa or Mastercard.

Accepted most places, but not all. Amex is hit-or-miss — carry a backup. Vendors rarely charge a card fee, and paying in Lempiras usually gives you a better rate. Use a card with no foreign-transaction fees, even paying in USD.

Tipping tip

Tell the server before they swipe.

Paying by card at restaurants, you usually need to tell your server the tip amount before they run the card — there's often no line to write it in. If you forget, cash tips are always welcome. Rule of thumb: ~15%.

Small bills

Your best friend in Roatán.

We always bring a stash of $1s and $5s — a few hundred dollars' worth — and it makes life so much easier. Local vendors and service staff really appreciate small bills for tips, snacks, taxis, and quick purchases.

Sales tax surprise

15%–19%, depending.

Sales tax in Roatán is steep. If a price doesn't include tax it can be a surprise at checkout — most vendors will tell you whether tax is baked in, but when in doubt, just ask. No one likes unexpected math on vacation.

★ The main event

Some of the best snorkeling in the world — just down the beach.

Walk out to the beach, turn left, and head all the way to the end where the rocks are. Enter the water and swim straight out to the reef, then go right. There's a huge reef that surrounds the island, and a big shelf with a drop-off. You can snorkel here for hours.

For little kids

Swimming with the fishies.

In the shallow water by the rocks, you can stand and be surrounded by colorful fish. For families with little ones, this is always the highlight — now anywhere we travel, our kids are disappointed when they're not surrounded by fish in shallow water.

Can't-miss

Scuba diving.

If you dive, you can't come to Roatán without diving. Extremely affordable; the reef is close so boat rides are short. Night dives, shipwrecks, drift dives — you name it. Favorite shop: West Bay Divers. Good reviews on Naboo & Infinity Divers too.

Day trip · weather-permitting

Cayos Cochinos.

Tiny remote islands about two hours away with some of the most pristine, untouched snorkeling we've ever seen. Most tours include a fresh fish lunch cooked for you by the locals. Not cheap — definitely worth it. Boats only go if the weather is good.

Boats · booze · snorkel

Mono Loco.

Snorkel tours, sunset tours, dinner cruises. The food and drinks are fantastic and the trips are super fun — music, dancing, partying. Kids loved it too.

Full-day excursion · East End Tour

Treasure Island Tiki Boat.

A full day exploring the wild east end of the island — from a tiki boat. Swim with sharks and stingrays, wade ashore at gorgeous Jade Beach, wind through the mangrove canals, and snorkel over a sunken shipwreck. One of those days you'll be talking about long after the tan fades.

+504 3168-6873 · www.roatantikiboat.com · Instagram @treasureislandtiki

Gumbalimba Park.

Right at the entrance to West Bay. Hold macaws, have monkeys jump on your head, see iguanas. A true sanctuary — the monkeys & macaws aren't caged. You sometimes see their macaws flying over West Bay.

Walking distance · family-friendly
